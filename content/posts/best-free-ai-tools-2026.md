Every week there's a new "best AI tools" list. Most of them read like press releases.

I took a different approach: I actually used these tools for three months. Here's what made it into my daily workflow.

---

## My Current Stack

**Claude** — main writing and coding assistant. The 200K context window matters more than you think. I've dumped entire codebases into it and it just... works.

**Cursor** — all my code editing. Took me a week to get used to, now I can't go back. The Composer feature (Cmd+I) lets me edit multiple files at once.

👉 [Try Cursor Free](https://cursor.sh/)

**Perplexity** — quick research and fact-checking. Better than Google for technical questions because it cites sources.

**ChatGPT 4o** — image analysis, brainstorming, debugging. The free tier gets you 10-20 GPT-4o messages every few hours.

**DeepSeek** — heavy batch processing when I don't want to hit limits elsewhere. Completely free and surprisingly capable.

---

## What Didn't Make the Cut

Not every hyped tool is worth your time:

- **Copilot** — fine for autocomplete, but Cursor does everything it does and more
- **Gemini** — improved a lot, but still inconsistent for coding
- **Jasper / Copy.ai** — these AI writing tools for marketers. If you're a developer or creator, Claude or ChatGPT does it better for free

---

## How I Actually Use These

A typical day looks like:

1. Morning: Perplexity to catch up on dev news and research
2. Mid-day: Cursor for coding, with Claude in the background for architecture questions
3. Afternoon: ChatGPT for debugging and image analysis
4. Evening: DeepSeek for batch processing or long-form analysis

Five tools, zero subscriptions. Well, I pay for Cursor Pro ($20/mo) but the free tier is generous enough for most people.

---

## The Bottom Line

You don't need to spend $200/month on AI tools. The free tiers of Claude, Cursor, Perplexity, ChatGPT, and DeepSeek cover 95% of what people actually do with AI.

Start with one, add more as you need them. Don't fall for the FOMO.
